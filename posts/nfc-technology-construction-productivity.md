## What is Near Field Communication (NFC)?
Near Field Communication (NFC) is a wireless communication technology that enables short-range communication between devices, typically within a few centimeters. This contactless communication method allows devices to exchange data by simply bringing them close together or tapping them. NFC operates on radio frequency identification (RFID) principles and is commonly used for various applications, including mobile payments, data transfer between smartphones, and contactless identification systems. The technology relies on two components: an active device, such as a smartphone or NFC-enabled card, and a passive device or reader that generates a magnetic field for communication. NFC has gained widespread popularity due to its simplicity, convenience, and versatility, making it an integral part of modern-day transactions, connectivity, and information exchange.

Near Field Communication (NFC) technology has been a game-changer across various industries, and its application in construction is no exception. The NFC-chip embedded in DashCards enables seamless communication between the card and smartphones, creating an efficient and reliable system for real-time data exchange.
